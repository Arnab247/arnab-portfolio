# Time Series Forecasting & Financial Machine Learning

**Role:** Undergraduate Researcher  
**Institution:** University of Manitoba, Department of Statistics  
**Supervisor:** Dr. A. Thavaneswaran  
**Core Technologies:** Machine Learning, Deep Learning, LSTM, SVM, KNN, Time Series Analysis

## Project Overview

Conducted foundational research on the application of advanced machine learning and deep learning models to complex time-series forecasting. The primary focus of this 7-month remote internship was evaluating and comparing the efficacy of various predictive algorithms when applied to volatile financial datasets, specifically stock market predictions.

## Key Contributions & Technical Depth

### 1. Deep Learning for Sequential Data
* **LSTM Architecture:** Investigated and applied Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural networks to capture long-term dependencies and hidden patterns within financial time-series data.
* **Predictive Modeling:** Built baseline models to forecast future price movements, understanding the nuances of sequence-to-sequence learning in highly stochastic environments.

### 2. Statistical Machine Learning Applications
* **Algorithm Evaluation:** Implemented and benchmarked traditional statistical machine learning models, including Support Vector Machines (SVM) and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), against deep learning approaches.
* **Comparative Analysis:** Analyzed the trade-offs between computational complexity, overfitting risks, and predictive accuracy among the different algorithmic frameworks.

### 3. Financial Data Engineering
* **Market Dynamics:** Developed a rigorous understanding of financial market data structures, learning how to preprocess, normalize, and format raw market feeds into viable training sets for machine learning models.
* **Research Methodology:** Gained early, hands-on experience in the academic research process, bridging the gap between theoretical statistics and applied predictive programming.
