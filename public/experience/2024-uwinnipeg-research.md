# High-Dimensional Statistical Regression Research

**Role:** Undergraduate Researcher  
**Institution:** The University of Winnipeg, Department of Mathematics and Statistics  
**Supervisor:** Dr. Shakhawat Hossain  
**Core Technologies:** R, Statistical Modeling, LASSO/Ridge Regression, High-Dimensional Data Analysis

## Project Overview

Conducted intensive statistical research focused on high-dimensional data analysis. The project centered on evaluating, optimizing, and comparing the performance of advanced regression models to identify the most effective techniques for handling complex datasets with a massive number of variables.

## Key Contributions & Technical Depth

### 1. Advanced Model Implementation
* **Algorithm Development:** Wrote robust R code to implement and evaluate multiple complex regression techniques, including LASSO, Ridge Regression, Elastic Net, Adaptive Lasso, and Group Lasso.
* **Performance Benchmarking:** Established a comparative framework to systematically test these novel high-dimensional data analysis techniques against one another on large-scale datasets.

### 2. Statistical Optimization & Analysis
* **Metric-Driven Evaluation:** Analyzed model performance utilizing rigorous statistical metrics, specifically targeting the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC), Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC), and R-Squared values.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Conducted precise variations of alpha parameters to ensure all regression models were fully mathematically optimized prior to their final performance comparisons.

### 3. Data Visualization
* **Graphical Analysis:** Created comprehensive graphical visualizations to map the behavior of the statistical models both before and after the optimization processes.
* **Quantitative Proof:** Showcased the concrete effectiveness of the parameter tuning, providing clear visual evidence of how optimization methods improve the accuracy of high-dimensional data models.
