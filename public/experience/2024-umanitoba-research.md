# Distributive Nonradiative Communication Research

**Role:** Undergraduate Researcher  
**Institution:** University of Manitoba, Department of Electrical Engineering  
**Supervisors:** Dr. Ekram Hossain (IEEE Fellow) & Dr. Amine Mezghani  
**Core Technologies:** MATLAB, Fourier Analysis, Mathematical Modeling, Signal Processing

## Project Overview

Engaged in advanced theoretical research focused on Distributive Nonradiative Communication systems under the supervision of leading experts in the field, including an IEEE Fellow. The work centered on validating complex mathematical models and translating theoretical signal processing concepts into functional, computational simulations.

## Key Contributions & Technical Depth

### 1. Mathematical Modeling & MATLAB Translation
* **Algorithm Implementation:** Translated complex, theoretical equations from academic manuscripts into highly optimized MATLAB code to simulate communication channel behaviors.
* **Fourier Domain Analysis:** Worked extensively within the Fourier domain to model and analyze signal transformations, requiring a deep understanding of frequency-domain representations and time-frequency duality.

### 2. Academic Validation & Proofreading
* **Mathematical Verification:** Conducted rigorous proofreading of research equations to ensure the mathematical integrity and accuracy of the proposed communication models prior to publication or further simulation.
* **Simulation Debugging:** Bridged the gap between abstract theory and computational reality, debugging MATLAB implementations to ensure the simulated outputs accurately reflected the physical layer theories being researched.

### 3. Advanced Signal Processing Foundations
* **Theoretical Grounding:** Developed a strong foundation in the theoretical underpinnings of modern communication systems, directly complementing practical coursework in signal processing and control systems.
* **Research Methodology:** Gained firsthand experience in the academic research pipeline, from theoretical derivation to computational validation, within a high-level university laboratory setting.
