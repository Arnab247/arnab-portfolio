# Statistical Regression Modeling: Cancer Mortality Prediction

**Role:** Undergraduate Researcher  
**Institution:** The University of Winnipeg, Department of Mathematics and Statistics  
**Supervisor:** Dr. Shakhawat Hossain  
**Core Technologies:** Statistical Regression, Data Modeling, Predictive Analytics

## Project Overview

Conducted remote statistical research focused on advanced regression techniques under the mentorship of Dr. Shakhawat Hossain. The core objective of the 6-month internship was to deeply understand statistical relationships in healthcare data, culminating in the development of a predictive data model designed to forecast cancer mortality rates across the United States.

## Key Contributions & Technical Depth

### 1. Predictive Data Modeling
* **Regression Analysis:** Studied and applied in-depth statistical regression methodologies to identify key variables, correlations, and trends within large-scale public health datasets.
* **Mortality Rate Prediction:** Built and refined a predictive statistical model specifically targeting US cancer mortality rates, successfully translating raw, complex health data into actionable quantitative insights.

### 2. Academic Research & Methodology
* **Mentored Learning:** Engaged in rigorous directed study to master foundational and advanced topics in statistical modeling and data analysis.
* **Quantitative Problem Solving:** Developed strong, fundamental research skills by structuring data sets, formulating analytical approaches, and validating the accuracy of the generated predictive models.
